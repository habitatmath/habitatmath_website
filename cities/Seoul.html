Seoul – Neon nights, mountain mornings, and 24-hour everything

City Beat

South Korea’s capital holds ~9.5M residents in the city, ~25M in the metro area. It’s a place where palaces sit under skyscrapers and cafés never seem to close.

The Han River splits the city into north and south; bridges light up after dark like a festival. Street life is active from dawn to deep night, with food stalls, boutiques, and late-night buses keeping the rhythm.

Seoul blends tradition and tech in a way that feels natural—tea houses near VR arcades, historic gates by LED billboards.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Winters are cold and crisp: highs −1–4 °C, sub-zero on ~70 days/yr. Snow falls 25–30 days/yr, often light but scenic.

Summers are warm and humid: highs 28–32 °C, with monsoon rains in July bringing ~350 mm that month alone. Annual rainfall totals ~1,450 mm.

Sunshine averages 2,000–2,200 h/yr. Spring and autumn are short but stunning, with cherry blossoms and crimson maples.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

A massive metro network: 23+ lines, 700+ stations, and ~1,200 km of track. Peak headways 2–4 min.

T-money cards cover subways, buses, and even taxis. Airport Express gets you from Incheon to Seoul Station in 43 min.

Bike-shares (Ddareungi) dot the city, while late-night buses fill in for metro closures.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Incheon (ICN) handles most international traffic; Gimpo (GMP) covers domestic and nearby. ICN–city by AREX express or all-stop train in under an hour.

Hotels: budget guesthouses ₩40,000–70,000/night, mid-range ₩90,000–150,000, luxury from ₩250,000+.

Street eats are cheap thrills: tteokbokki ₩3,000–5,000, gimbap ₩2,000–4,000, café coffee ₩4,500–6,000.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed ₩800,000–1.2M/mo; outer districts ₩500,000–800,000. Utilities ₩150,000–250,000/mo.

Groceries for two ₩500,000–700,000/mo. Internet is blazing fast at ₩25,000–40,000/mo.

Green spaces like Namsan and Bukhansan keep fitness free. Public safety is high—midnight walks feel normal.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Violent crime is very low; petty theft rare. Health care is modern and affordable, with international clinics in key districts.

Education quality is strong; English-speaking schools cater to expats. Immigration services are organized but expect queues.

Visa renewals are predictable—documents in order speed things up.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Try the Han River picnic with delivery fried chicken. Explore palaces early to beat tour crowds.

Metro is your best friend; taxis are affordable for groups at night. Always keep some cash for market stalls.

Avoid peak cherry blossom weekends unless you love crowds.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Choose your neighborhood by vibe: Hongdae for youth, Gangnam for polish, Seongbuk for quiet charm.

Learn basic Korean—it goes a long way. Get a local bank account early to smooth online payments.

Weekend hikes keep city stress in check; invest in good walking shoes.
