Bengaluru – Startups, filter coffee, and traffic that tests zen

City Beat

India’s tech capital houses ~12M residents and a metro sprawl of ~8,000 km².

Known for its IT parks, tree-lined streets, and unpredictable weather.

A melting pot of cultures, cuisines, and caffeine-fueled ambition.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Mild year-round: summer highs around 28–33 °C, winter lows 15–18 °C.

Rainfall peaks June–September with ~970 mm/year.

Sunshine averages ~2,900 h/year—plenty for rooftop gardens.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

Namma Metro spans 73 km, expanding fast with new lines.

BMTC buses: 6,000+ fleet covering all corners.

Monthly passes: Metro ₹1,800, buses ₹1,050.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Kempegowda Airport links to 80+ destinations, 40 km from city center.

Budget stays ₹1,200–2,000/night, mid-range ₹3,000–5,000.

South Indian breakfast ₹50–150, craft beer ₹250–400.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed rents ₹25–40k/mo, suburban ₹15–25k.

Internet ₹700–1,200/mo, utilities ₹2–4k.

QOL strong for weather and dining, weak for traffic flow.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Safety index 60–65; safe at night in main areas.

Strong healthcare sector—private hospitals rank nationally.

Moderate bureaucracy—property rentals need verified agreements.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Stroll Cubbon Park early morning—it’s bliss.

Try authentic filter coffee at heritage cafés.

Use ride-hailing apps—autos can overcharge visitors.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Live close to work—traffic eats hours daily.

Join local sports or hobby clubs—great networking.

Consider suburbs like Whitefield or HSR for quieter life.
