Kyōto – Temples, tea, and timeless streets

City Pulse

Japan’s cultural capital with 1.45 million residents.

Home to 17 UNESCO sites and more than 1,600 temples.

AQI often under 25. Streets hum with history and bicycles.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Winters: 1–9 °C, with 15 sub-zero days and light snow.

Summers: 28–33 °C, humid, and sunny 2,000 h yearly.

Rain peaks in June–July with 1,600 mm total yearly.

🚊 Public Transit Snapshot

Two subway lines and 7 rail companies serve the city.

Buses cover 100+ routes, but can be slow in peak season.

Day pass: ¥900 for buses, ¥1,200 for combined rail.

🏨 Short Stays & Getting There

Shinkansen from Tokyo: 2 hr 20 min. Osaka Airport to Kyoto: 80 min.

Budget stay: ¥4,500–6,000. Ryokan mid-range: ¥12,000–20,000.

Street food: ¥500–1,000. Kaiseki dinner: ¥8,000+.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1-bed central: ¥70,000–90,000/mo. Outer: ¥50,000–65,000.

Utilities: ¥10,000–15,000. Internet 100 Mbps+ for ¥4,500.

Clean streets, punctual transit, and low noise levels.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Very safe; petty crime rare.

Excellent healthcare and a strong school network.

Visa extensions and city registration are straightforward with guidance.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Visit Fushimi Inari at dawn. Avoid buses midday in high season.

Rent a bike to explore quieter temples.

Try matcha parfaits—they’re addictive.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Learn basic Japanese—it unlocks deeper social ties.

Shop at Nishiki Market early for fresh picks.

Invest in a seasonal wardrobe—Kyōto’s summers and winters are extremes.
