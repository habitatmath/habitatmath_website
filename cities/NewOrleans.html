New Orleans – Jazz in the Streets, Spice in the Air

City Pulse

The Big Easy hums with 370,000 residents and millions of visitors yearly.

Birthplace of jazz, home to 130+ festivals annually.

Sits below sea level, guarded by 560 km of levees.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Mild winters: 7–18 °C, rare frost days (2–3/year).

Hot summers: 31–34 °C, 2,600 h sunshine.

Rain: 1,600 mm yearly, hurricane risk late summer.

🚊 Public Transit Snapshot

Streetcars on 4 lines, buses cover 30+ routes.

Monthly pass: $55, ferries cross the Mississippi for $2.

Walkability score: 59, bike score: 68.

🏨 Short Stays & Getting There

Louis Armstrong Airport 20 min from French Quarter.

Budget stay: $90–130, mid-range: $150–210.

Beignets: $4–6, po’boys: $9–14.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1-bed central: $1,400–1,700/mo, outer: $950–1,200.

Utilities: $130. Internet 200 Mbps+ for $55.

Culture and cuisine boost QOL; flooding risk tempers it.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Petty crime possible—stay alert in tourist zones.

Good hospitals, several universities.

City paperwork is slow; plan ahead.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Avoid Bourbon Street after midnight if you value sleep.

Ride the St. Charles streetcar for history and scenery.

Sample gumbo in more than one place—it’s never the same twice.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Keep flood insurance active.

Embrace festivals; they define the city.

Learn hurricane prep like a local.
