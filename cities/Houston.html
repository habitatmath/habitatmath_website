Houston – Space City with barbecue-fueled gravity

City Beat

Home to ~2.3M residents in city limits and ~7M in the metro. The skyline sprawls; so do freeways. NASA’s Johnson Space Center keeps eyes upward while the port keeps goods flowing.

AQI swings from 30–70 depending on season and storm dust. Diverse neighborhoods: Montrose arts, Midtown nightlife, Heights bungalows, and Pearland’s calm.

Big on energy, bigger on food—Tex-Mex, Viet-Cajun crawfish, and brisket that demands a nap.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Summers are bold: highs 32–38 °C with humidity hugging 70–80%. Winters mild: lows 5–10 °C, sub-zero days rare.

Rain totals ~1,300–1,400 mm/yr spread over 100–110 days; tropical storms can double that in a week. Sunshine: 2,500–2,700 h/yr.

Snow is a curiosity—maybe 1 day/5 years. Spring and autumn are short but perfect.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

METRORail covers ~37 km in three lines; buses do the heavy lifting. Park-and-ride links suburbs to downtown.

Monthly passes: $50–75. Headways 6–15 min on light rail peak hours.

Freeways define commutes—expect 30–50 min if you drive. Cycling infrastructure: improving but patchy.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Two airports: IAH (international) and HOU (domestic-heavy). IAH is ~37 km from downtown; METRO bus or ride-share in 35–50 min.

Hotels: budget $80–120/night, mid-range $140–200, luxe $250+. Tacos: $2–4 each; brisket plate $15–25.

Daily visitor spend: $80–150 depending on indulgence.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1-bed central $1,300–1,800/mo; outer suburbs $950–1,300. Utilities $150–250/mo—summer A/C is a budget line.

Grocery costs: $300–450/mo per person. Roads wide, parking easy. Water drainage: mixed performance in heavy storms.

QOL shines with a yard and a BBQ pit.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Safety index mid 50s; varies by district. Texas Medical Center is world-class.

Universities: Rice, UH. City bureaucracy is straightforward; property taxes notable.

Hurricane season awareness is part of the local skillset.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Space Center, Museum District, and a rodeo if you can time it. BBQ tours should be paced—brisket has mass.

Downtown tunnels are air-conditioned lifelines in summer.

Galveston beaches are a quick getaway.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Flood maps matter for home choice. Commute corridors decide your stress level.

Embrace A/C maintenance and hurricane kits.

Get into local food festivals—Houston eats globally.
