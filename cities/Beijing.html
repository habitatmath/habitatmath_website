Beijing – Imperial walls, modern malls, and big-sky ambition

City Pulse

China’s capital runs on scale and ceremony. City proper houses ~22M residents; the metro region stretches far beyond. Axial avenues meet hutong alleys; courtyards sit behind red doors, ring roads loop like orbits.

AQI swings with seasons—blue-sky streaks are common now, yet winter inversions can nudge AQI 80–150. Parks are plentiful: Temple of Heaven dawn tai chi, Beihai boats by afternoon. Museums, tech zones, and a restaurant scene that spans every province.

It’s orderly, fast, and surprisingly green in pockets. If you like structure with side quests, this capital delivers.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Continental flair. Winters are crisp: highs −3–5 °C, sub-zero mornings on 60–80 days/yr. Snow is light but photogenic—5–10 days/yr.

Summers go warm-to-hot: highs 29–34 °C, short heatwaves above 35 °C. Annual precipitation around 600 mm, concentrated in July–August storms.

Sunshine is generous at 2,500–2,800 h/yr. Spring can be dusty; autumn is perfection—golden ginkgos, blue skies, light sweaters.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

One of the world’s largest metros: 25+ lines, 700+ km, 400+ stations. Peak headways 2–3 min. Tap with a transit card or mobile pay.

Buses blanket the gaps; BRT runs key corridors. Daxing Intl to the city via the Airport Express in 19–30 min depending on destination. Taxis are metered and plentiful.

Cycling is back in style—shared bikes on most corners. Commuter punctuality is a point of pride.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Two main airports: PEK (Capital) and PKX (Daxing). High-speed rails fan to Tianjin (30–40 min), Shanghai (4.5–5.5 h).

Hotels span ranges: business mid-range ¥500–900/night, luxury ¥1,500+. Courtyard boutiques in hutongs are mood-forward.

Daily eats scale fast: breakfast jianbing ¥8–15, noodles ¥25–45, Peking duck dinner for two ¥200–400. Coffee ¥20–35.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed ¥7,000–12,000/mo; outer districts ¥4,500–7,000. Utilities (seasonal heat/AC) ¥400–800/mo. Home internet ¥120–200/mo.

Groceries for two ¥1,800–2,800/mo. Commutes average 35–60 min across rings. Parks and lakes punctuate density; sidewalks invite evening walks.

Digital payments everywhere; delivery logistics are wizard-level. QOL jumps if you live near a metro + park combo.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Very low violent crime; petty theft uncommon but stay aware in crowds. Hospitals are abundant; international clinics shorten wait times.

Schools range from local key schools to international campuses. Health insurance with direct-billing clinics is worth it.

Residence permits and registrations are process-heavy—appointments, photos, copies. Once set, life runs smoothly.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

The Great Wall: arrive early, pick a section (Mutianyu for views, Jinshanling for hikes). Forbidden City tickets sell out—book ahead. Ride Line 2 for a DIY ring-city sampler.

Slurp zhajiangmian in a hutong, then tea-house hop near Shichahai. Badge and scan culture is normal—carry ID and power bank.

Autumn is peak. Winter has palace vibes with fewer elbows; wear real layers.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Live by your line, not your map—two transfers beat a long bus. Learn a few Mandarin phrases; service life accelerates.

Air days vary—keep a small purifier and check forecasts. Embrace delivery for bulky items; use lockers for packages.

Weekend trains unlock Hebei hikes and hot springs. Save your favorite noodle shop in maps; it will vanish behind a new facade and reappear two doors down.
