Gothenburg – Trams, seafood, and a dash of Nordic chill

City Beat

Sweden’s second city holds ~580k residents and a metro of ~1M.

Industrial roots meet cozy café culture along canals and cobbled streets.

A maritime hub with some of Scandinavia’s best seafood.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Cool summers: highs 20–23 °C, mild winters -2 to 4 °C.

Snowfall ~35 days/year, sub-zero days ~50.

Rainy reputation—~160 days/year—pack waterproofs.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

Tram network: 160 km, iconic blue-and-white fleet.

Buses fill in gaps, ferries link islands.

Monthly pass SEK 795 covers all modes.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Landvetter Airport serves 90+ destinations.

Hotels: budget SEK 900–1,200/night, mid-range SEK 1,500–2,000.

Seafood platters SEK 250–400, coffee SEK 35–50.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed rents SEK 10–13k/mo, suburbs SEK 8–10k.

Utilities SEK 900–1,300/mo, internet SEK 300–400/mo.

Excellent cycling paths, efficient healthcare, and low pollution.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Safety index 70–75; some petty crime in nightlife zones.

Public healthcare accessible for residents—high quality.

Straightforward paperwork for EU/EEA citizens.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Visit Liseberg amusement park—fun year-round.

Explore Southern Archipelago—no cars, pure charm.

Warm up with fika on rainy afternoons.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Learn some Swedish—it smooths daily interactions.

Invest in quality rain gear—it’s a lifestyle need.

Consider cycling for short commutes—it’s fast and scenic.
