Los Angeles – Sunshine, freeways, and cinematic daydreams

City Beat

LA sprawls with ~3.8M residents in the city, ~13M metro-wide.

Entertainment capital, home to Hollywood, tech hubs, and endless beaches.

Diversity is the default—over 180 languages spoken.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Sunshine reigns with ~284 sunny days/year.

Summer highs 28–32 °C, winter lows 8–12 °C.

Rainfall minimal—~380 mm/year—mostly Dec–Mar.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

Metro Rail covers 170 km, expanding steadily.

Monthly pass $50—a bargain if you skip the car.

Bike lanes: ~1,200 km, but drivers rule the road.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

LAX connects to 180+ destinations.

Budget stays $80–120/night, mid-range $160–250.

Street tacos $2–4, latte $4–6.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed rents $2,000–2,800/mo.

Utilities $150–250/mo, internet $50–70/mo.

World-class airports, entertainment, and dining—but traffic is infamous.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Safety index 55–60—varies heavily by neighborhood.

Strong university presence: UCLA, USC.

Simple bureaucracy for US residents, complex for visas.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Avoid rush hour—your trip can double in time.

Visit Griffith Observatory for skyline + Hollywood Sign view.

Explore neighborhoods—each feels like a different city.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Choose housing near work—commutes are legendary.

Learn neighborhood safety maps—they matter.

Tap into local farmers markets—they’re fresh and social.
