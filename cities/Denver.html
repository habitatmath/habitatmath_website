Denver – Mile-High skies, sunshine surplus, mountain minutes

City Pulse

The Mile-High City sits at 1,609 m with ~715,000 residents; the metro passes ~3.0M. Red-brick warehouses became coffee labs, breweries, and galleries. Rockies on the horizon set a daily mood.

AQI is usually 25–45, with occasional wildfire haze in late summer. Downtown-to-neighborhoods is a quick hop: LoDo lively, RiNo artsy, Wash Park jogger nation.

Sport is civic religion. So are patios. Sunshine shows up like it’s on payroll.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Semi-arid snap. Winter highs 5–10 °C, nights dip below freezing on 60–90 days/yr. Annual snowfall averages 120–150 cm with 25–30 snow days.

Summers run 28–33 °C; heat waves over 35 °C happen but are dry. Sunshine boasts 3,000+ h/yr. Annual precipitation sits around 350–400 mm.

Spring can snow, then melt by lunch. Fall is leaf-glow + perfect tailgate weather.

🚊 Public Transit Snapshot

RTD runs light rail + commuter rail + buses. The A-Line airport train hits Union Station in ~37 min with 15–30 min headways.

Monthly passes range about $88–$176 by zone. Frequent bus corridors thread most neighborhoods.

Bike network keeps growing—200+ miles of lanes/trails. Scooters fill the last mile.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

DEN is big and busy, ~40 km from downtown. Trains are easiest; rideshare takes 30–45 min off-peak.

Hotels: mid-range $150–220/night, boutique/luxe from $280+. Hostels/private rooms $60–110.

Tacos $3–5 each, craft beer pint $6–8, solid dinner for two $70–110. Day budget without Broncos tickets: $90–160.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1-bed central $1,800–2,400/mo; outside core $1,400–1,900. Utilities with winter heat $130–200/mo. Internet 300–1,000 Mbps for $50–80.

Groceries for two $600–900/mo. Commutes average 20–40 min. Tap water is crisp; parks and trails web the city.

QOL shines if you like blue skies, sports, and weekend mountain escapes. Snow plows earn their keep; streets rebound fast.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Generally safe with downtown hot spots; typical big-city awareness applies. Hospitals and urgent care are widespread.

Universities anchor research and arts. Paperwork standard; front-range counties run services efficiently.

Altitude is real—hydrate early week one. Sunscreen isn’t optional at 1,609 m.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Start at Union Station, then stroll the 16th Street spine. Golden Triangle museums on a breezy afternoon; Red Rocks for sunset if shows align.

Day trips: Golden breweries, Boulder hikes, Georgetown Loop Railroad. If it snows, wait two hours—then watch streets reappear.

Book ski shuttles ahead; I-70 traffic has its own mythology. Brunch? Lines decide—arrive early, or eat tacos first.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Live near your preferred corridor—A-Line, Colfax frequent bus, or a bike greenway. Choose heat type wisely; winters test furnaces.

Keep an emergency trunk kit: scraper, gloves, blanket. Learn the snow-parking rules by heart.

Build a weekend mountain rhythm: early out, early back. And yes, everyone owns microspikes—join the club.
