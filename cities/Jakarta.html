Jakarta – Traffic jams, street snacks, and endless energy

City Beat

Indonesia’s sprawling capital is home to ~11M residents in the city and ~33M in the metro—one of the world’s largest urban areas.

A mix of skyscrapers, kampungs, malls, and markets; the pace never slows.

Jakarta is economic muscle and political nerve center, with a social life that spills into streets, malls, and cafés.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Tropical monsoon climate: highs 30–32 °C year-round, lows 24–26 °C.

Wet season (Nov–Mar) brings ~300 mm/month rainfall; annual total ~2,000 mm.

Sunshine averages ~2,800 h/yr, with humidity 80–85%.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

MRT Jakarta: 16 km first line, expanding. Commuter trains (KRL) link metro with suburbs.

TransJakarta BRT system is massive: 250+ km dedicated lanes. Ojeks (motorbike taxis) weave through jams.

E-payment cards cover most modes; Grab and Gojek dominate ride-hailing.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Soekarno–Hatta International Airport (CGK) is main entry; rail link to downtown in 45 min.

Hotels: budget Rp250,000–400,000/night, mid-range Rp600,000–1,200,000, luxury from Rp2.5M+.

Street eats: nasi goreng Rp15,000–25,000, bakso soup Rp10,000–20,000, kopi Rp5,000–15,000.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed Rp4.5M–7M/mo; outer areas Rp2.5M–4.5M. Utilities Rp800k–1.2M/mo.

Internet Rp300k–500k/mo. QOL affected by traffic—choose location wisely.

New MRT extensions promise relief, but most residents still rely on motorbikes and BRT.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Petty theft in crowded spots—keep valuables close. Violent crime is rare.

Healthcare ranges from basic public hospitals to high-end private clinics. English-speaking services limited outside major facilities.

Visa on arrival for many nationals (30 days), extendable once.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Avoid peak-hour travel; even short distances can take an hour.

Explore Old Batavia for colonial charm, then dive into modern malls for AC relief.

Street food is safe if cooked hot and fresh.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Live close to your workplace—commuting can eat hours daily.

Use motorbike taxis for speed, but wear a helmet.

Prepare for seasonal flooding in certain neighborhoods.
