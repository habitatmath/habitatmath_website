Prague – Spires, cobbles, beer that tastes like history

City Pulse

Prague blends Gothic drama with café calm. City population sits near 1.3M; the metro swells to 2.6M+. The Vltava splits neighborhoods into postcard angles. Old Town and Malá Strana pull cameras like magnets.

Air quality stays decent at AQI ~25–40. Tourism ebbs and flows; shoulder seasons feel best. Beer is culture, not just a drink—pints often under CZK 60.

Safety index is high, petty theft near hotspots. Walkability is strong; you can cross the core in 25–35 min. Tram bells set the soundtrack.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Four seasons, each with a mood. Winters bring 20–35 sub-zero days and occasional snow on 15–25 days/yr. January averages around −1–0 °C.

Summers are warm, rarely brutal: highs 24–28 °C, heat spikes above 32 °C on a handful of days. Rainfall totals about 520–600 mm/yr over 110–130 wet days.

Sunshine clocks roughly 1,650–1,800 h/yr. Spring and autumn are the sweet spot: mild air, golden light, fewer crowds.

🚊 Trams, Metro & Trains

Three metro lines cover ~65 km with 60+ stations. Peak headways hit 2–3 min. On-time rates hover near 95%+.

Trams are the star: 25+ lines, great night network, core headways 3–6 min. A 30-min ticket is CZK 30, 90-min CZK 40, day pass CZK 120.

Prague main station links Vienna in ~4 h, Berlin in ~4.5 h. Airport bus to metro in 15–20 min.

🏨 Short Stays & Getting There

Václav Havel Airport sits ~17 km from center; AE bus runs ~35 min to hlavní nádraží. Taxis/ride-hail to Old Town: CZK 500–800.

Hostels from CZK 350–600/night. Mid-range hotels CZK 2,200–3,600. Boutique/luxe from CZK 5,000+ in peak.

Daily visitor budget without splurges: CZK 1,200–2,000. Coffee CZK 55–80. Hearty lunch menus CZK 150–250.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

Central 1-bed rent: CZK 25,000–35,000/mo. Outside center: CZK 18,000–25,000. Utilities for a small flat: CZK 3,000–4,500. Internet 300–1000 Mbps for CZK 400–700.

Groceries for two: CZK 7,000–10,000/mo. Commute times average 25–40 min. Tap water is excellent. Parks and river walks are close to most districts.

QOL runs high: quiet residential nights (~50–55 dB), strong arts, good healthcare access. Bring walking shoes; cobbles are relentless.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Low violent crime, pickpockets in tourist zones—basic vigilance works. Police and EMS are responsive. Pharmacies are ubiquitous.

Public and private hospitals serve well; English coverage in major facilities is solid. Universities host 100k+ students across the city.

Bureaucracy likes appointments and documents in order. Residence processes vary by nationality; expect 2–6 weeks for basics.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Charles Bridge at sunrise, not noon. Ride tram #22 for a rolling city tour. Book castle precincts early; lines stretch fast.

Try chimney cake? Fun—but look for local koláče and svíčková for true flavor. Swap Old Town bars for Vinohrady or Holešovice.

Validate tickets, always. Carry small cash for kiosks. Watch cobbles in rain—grip beats glamour.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Live near a tram/metro node; winter will thank you. Check heating type before signing. South-facing windows lift winter mood.

Learn a few Czech phrases; service life gets easier. Batch errands—post, pharmacy, bakery—within one block like a local.
