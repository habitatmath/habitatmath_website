Bali – Temples, surf, and sunsets with extra magic

Island Pulse & First Look

Bali is Indonesia’s postcard come to life. Population ≈ 4.3M. Beaches, rice terraces, and volcano silhouettes.

Tourism is a major vein; Ubud hums with yoga mats, Canggu with surfboards. Culture is layered—temples, ceremonies, offerings on sidewalks.

The island moves at two speeds: serene mornings and scooter chaos. Weather sets the rhythm; offerings set the tone.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Tropical and warm year-round. Daily highs ≈ 27–31 °C. Humidity ~75–85%.

Dry season May–Sept, wet season Oct–Apr. Rainy days ≈ 90–130/yr. Sunshine 2,500+ h/yr.

Sea temps ~27–29 °C all year. Volcanic areas run cooler. Afternoon showers are common in wet season.

🚍 Public Transport

Buses are limited; tourist shuttles fill gaps. Scooters dominate—rent IDR 60k–90k/day.

Ride-hailing apps like Gojek and Grab are key. Traffic is dense in south Bali; travel times stretch.

Airport in Denpasar is central to tourist zones. Ferries link to nearby islands. Roads can be narrow and winding inland.

🏨 Short Stays & Daily Costs

Mid-range hotels IDR 600k–1.2M/night. Villas from IDR 900k. Hostels IDR 150k–300k.

Meals: warung lunch IDR 25k–50k. Mid-range dinner for two IDR 250k–400k. Coffee IDR 25k–40k.

Scooter fuel ~IDR 10k/litre. Attractions IDR 30k–150k. Day trips vary widely by guide and distance.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1BR rents IDR 6–12M/mo in tourist zones; less inland. Groceries for two IDR 3–5M/mo.

Internet 20–100 Mbps for IDR 400k–800k. Power outages happen; backup helps.

Healthcare ranges from local clinics to international hospitals. Roads are mixed; rural areas can be slow.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Bureaucracy

Petty theft exists; secure your gear. Driving requires care—chaotic traffic norms.

International schools serve expats. Visas need regular extension unless on long-term permit.

Healthcare in tourist areas is good but costly without insurance. Avoid tap water.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Visit temples with respect—cover shoulders and knees. Avoid busy beaches for sunrise magic.

Use offline maps; signals drop inland. Exchange money only at reputable spots.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Pick housing near daily needs; traffic is real. Learn basic Bahasa for smoother living.

Respect cultural days of silence and ceremony. Balance tourist zones with quiet inland escapes.
