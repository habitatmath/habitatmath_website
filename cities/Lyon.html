Lyon – France’s silk city with a gourmet addiction

City Pulse

France’s third largest city holds ~530k residents and a metro of ~2.4M. Two rivers, two hills, and endless bouchons define it.

AQI usually 20–35. Presqu’île bustles, Vieux Lyon whispers history, and Confluence experiments with modernity.

It’s the gastronomic capital—menus here read like poetry for carnivores.

🌤 Climate & Seasons

Winters 2–8 °C, ~15–20 sub-zero days, occasional snow 4–6 days/yr.

Summers warm: highs 26–32 °C, occasional heat above 35 °C. Annual sunshine ~2,000 h.

Rainfall ~830 mm, spread fairly evenly; spring and autumn mild and bright.

🚇 Public Transit Snapshot

TCL network: 4 metro lines, 2 funiculars, 5 tram lines, 140+ bus routes.

Monthly pass €69.40. Airport tram Rhônexpress to Part-Dieu in ~30 min.

City bike system Vélo’v offers >5,000 bikes at >400 stations.

🏨 Short Stays, Getting There & Daily Eats

Saint-Exupéry Airport ~25 km out; high-speed TGV to Paris in ~2 h.

Hotels: budget €70–110/night, mid-range €120–180, luxe €250+.

Set lunch menu €18–25, praline tart slice €3–5.

💰 Living Costs, QOL & Infrastructure

1-bed central €850–1,200/mo, outer €650–900. Utilities €100–160/mo.

Commutes 20–35 min. Green lungs: Parc de la Tête d’Or and river quays.

QOL is top-tier for food, culture, and access to Alps and Provence.

🛡 Safety, Health, Education & Paperwork

Safety index 70+. Public healthcare excellent; private clinics fast.

Universities: Lyon 1, 2, 3 and ENS attract global students.

Administration efficient by French standards—book appointments online.

🧭 Tips for Tourists

Try a bouchon, ride the funicular, and stroll traboules (hidden passages).

Visit Musée des Confluences for modern flair. Summer evenings along the Rhône are magic.

Winter lights festival (Fête des Lumières) is pure spectacle.

🏡 Tips for Long-Term Dwellers

Choose riverside or hillside living based on mobility preference. Shop at Croix-Rousse markets for local produce.

Embrace cycling—Vélo’v is cheap and reliable. Weekend getaways to Alps in ~2 h.

Keep a list of favorite bouchons; you’ll be asked for recommendations often.
