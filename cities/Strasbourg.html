🧭 Tips for Tourists

Don’t just stop at the cathedral — climb it. The view is worth the 332 steps. Skip taxis; trams and bikes are faster and far cheaper. A €4.60 day pass covers unlimited tram and bus rides. If you’re visiting in December, the Christmas markets here are among Europe’s oldest, dating back to 1570. Try flammekueche at a riverside café for under €10. Boat tours along the Ill River cost about €14 and give you a crash course in city history.

Photography tip — go early to Petite France for empty streets and golden light. Most museums are free on the first Sunday of the month. Don’t miss Parc de l’Orangerie if you want to spot the city’s resident storks. Carry a rain jacket; there are 160 rainy days per year. Strasbourg’s old town is compact — you can walk the whole UNESCO zone in under 40 minutes.

Language hack: locals appreciate a “Guten Tag” almost as much as a “Bonjour.” Watch out for cobblestones — heels and thin bicycle tires don’t mix well. And yes, the canals are as photogenic as they look on postcards.
